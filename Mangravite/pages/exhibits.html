Exhbitions

During Mangravites career he participated in many exhibits include over 45 solo exhibitions.

  • 1921 Soceity of Independent Artists
  • 1922 Soceity of Independent Artists
  • 1926 Sesquicentennial Exposition in Philadelphia (Gold Medal)
  • 1928 Soceity of Independent Artists
  • 1929 Solo Exhibit at Dudensing Galleries, New York
  • 1930 Soceity of Independent Artists
  • 1931 Solo Exhibit "Paintings by Peppino Mangravate" at Dudensing Galleries, New York, from November 15th to December 4th.
  • 1934 Solo Exhibit at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York (March?)
  • 1937 Solo Exhibit at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York
  • 1938 Venice Biennale in Venice, Italy
  • 1939 The New York World’s Fair (received prize)
  • 1939 The Golden Gate Exposition (Gold Medal Purchase Prize for "Dancing in the Moonlight")
  • 1939 Section exhibit of mural designs at the Corcoran Art Gallery, November 1st to 21st
  • 1943 Woodmere Art Gallery
  • 1944 Solo Exhibit, "Song of the Poet, Peppino Mangravite; Paintings, Gouaches, Drawings" at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York, from January 31st to February 26th.
  • 1945 Los Angeles Couny Museum of History, Science and Art Drawing Biennial (sent "Girl with Bouquet")
  • 1947 National Academy of Design
  • 1951 Solo Exhibit "New Paintings by Peppino Mangravite", at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York from March 26th to April 14th.
  • 1953 Solo Exhibit at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York
  • 1955 Architecture League of New York (Silver Medal for mosaic design)
  • 1959 Solo Exhibit at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York
  • 1961 Solo Exhibit at Frank Rehn Gallery, New York
  • 1964 Century Association from November 10th to January 8th, 1965. One paintings "Lightening"
  • 1978 Art for the People - New Deal Murals on Long Island from Novemeber 1 to December 31 at the Emily Lowe Gallery at Hofstra University, New York
    • Sketches for both the Hempstead and the Jackson Heights murals were included in the exhibit orgainzed by David Shapiro
  • 1982 Solo Exhbit from July 19th to September 26th at the Burchfield Center in Buffalo, New York.
    • A catelog from the exhibit can be found in the Peppino Mangravite Papers at the Smithsonian Archives of American Art. The exhibit included items from Mangravite's daughters own collection as well as pieces from Munson-Williams-Proctor Institure, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Phillips Collection and the Whitney Museum of Art.

In the 1930s and 1940s Mangravite was include in exhibitions at many prestigious museums including the Art Institute of Chicago (15 times), the Museum of Modern Art, the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts (19 times), the Carnegie Institute, Corcoran Gallery (Biennials 10 times) and the Whitney Museum of American Art (15 times).

Other exhibit venues include;

  • Washington Water Color Club
  • Cincinnati Art Museum
  • Nebraska Art Association
  • Society of Washington Artists
  • Salons of America
