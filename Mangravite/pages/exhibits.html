    • His one man exhibitions with Rehn were in 1934, 1937, 1944, 1951, 1953, 1959, 1961. Between 1929 and 1932 Mangravite began to experiment with his own unique style. He soon realized that painterly fluidity and lyrical themes interest him most. In 1938 he was invited to exhibit at the Venice Biennale in Italy. In 1939 he exhibited at the New York World’s Fair and the Golden Gate Exposition, and took prizes at both. As a WPA artist, Mangravite won many mural commissions. H executed murals for Post Offices in Hempstead, Long Island, 1936, Atlantic City, 1939, and Jackson Heights, Queens, 1940. Mangravite also executed a mural for the Governor’s Mansion in the Virgin Islands in 1942. In the late 1940s Mangravite was include in exhibitions at many prestigious museums including the Art Institute of Chicago, the Museum of Modern Art, the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts, the Carnegie Institute, and the Whitney Museum of American Art.
